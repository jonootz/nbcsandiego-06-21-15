Assignment

Communication 105 / Summer 2015

News Analysis

Due: June 26

This assignment has four tasks.

Task #1: You must obtain one edition of a the UT San Diego, LA Times or The Press Enterprise between June18-23. You need to purchase a print copy to do this assignment. If you live outside of San Diego County – contact me with your local metropolitan daily.

 

Task #2: You must access one local TV news Web on the day you purchase your newspaper. You need to look at the home page news and the local news tab. You should analyze 20 stories.

http://www.nbcsandiego.com

http://www.cbs8.com

http://fox5sandiego.com

 

Task #3: Look at race, gender, age, disability and other differences for every article and photograph in the newspaper (except for the classified, real estate & automotive sections) and the lead and local news on the TV news Web sites (look at, at least 20 stories). Track the numbers on the spreadsheets provided and count the bylines (who wrote story), photography (who is pictured) and story count (who is the story about) for the newspaper and Web site. Do not include advertisements in your counts.

 

Task #4: Once you have filled out the spreadsheets for both -- use the information you gathered to type a 3-4 page analysis of your findings that answer the questions below. Make sure to include actual examples from the newspaper and Web site to support your findings.

 

1) What do you think about the newspaper and Web site’s overall coverage in terms of race and gender? Provide some examples to support your opinions.

2) How did the newspaper and Web site do with reflecting diversity/avoiding stereotypes in photographs/video? Were there a variety of people in the photos/video? Were the images stereotypical? If not, how were they un-stereotypical? Provide examples to support your opinions.

3) How did the newspaper and news site do with reflecting diversity/avoiding stereotypes in the story content (i.e. story about illegal immigrants? Provide examples to support your opinions.

4) Define Clark’s four stages of representation (in your reading). What stages did the ethnic groups fall in your newspaper and web site?

5) Look at the bylines of the stories (who wrote the stories). What can you tell about the staff’s makeup? Does gender affect what sections / types of news a reporter writes for? Provide examples to support your opinions.

6) Overall, which one – the newspaper or Web site did a better job of reflecting the community’s diversity and shattering stereotypes? Why? Which one perpetuated stereotypes?

7) Do you think the gatekeepers at these news organizations did a good job determining the most important stories of the day? Why or why not?

No late assignments are accepted. You must turn in the paper and count sheets on Blackboard by the end of the day on June 26.
